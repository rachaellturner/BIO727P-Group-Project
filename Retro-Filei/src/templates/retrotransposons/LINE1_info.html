LINE-1 elements make up 17% of the human genome and are the only known retrotransposons that are able to mediate their own mobility, but many are inactive.

LINE-1 retrotransposons can be identified by their genetic elements:

- An untranslated region containing an RNA polymerase II promoter.

- ORF0, a human specific product found in the antisense orientation.

- ORF1, which contains an RNA polymerase binding site.

- ORF2, which encodes an endonuclease and reverse transcription.

The promoter in the untranslated region encourages RNA polymerase II to bind at the ORF1 domain, the reverse transcriptase encoded by ORF2 allows a DNA copy of the transposon to be produced from the RNA strand and the endonuclease facilitates its insertion back into the genome.
