HERV elements make up approximately 10% of the human genome, but arenâ€™t active in transposition due to mutations and deletions.

Although not active, they have biologically active open reading frames, the products of which have been linked to carcinogenesis:

- GAG encodes for the retroviral capsid.

- POL encodes enzymes for viral replication, integration and protein cleavage.

- ENV encodes for envelope proteins

HERVs are classified based on their homology to animal retroviruses, but due to their complexity this can be difficult.
