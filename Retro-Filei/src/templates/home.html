Around 42% of the human genome is made up of retrotransposons.


These genomic elements are able to copy themselves via an RNA intermediate and are reinserted somewhere else into the genome sequence.

This method of transposition causes genomic enlargement and induces mutations by inserting near or within genes.

Accumulation of mutations means that many of these retrotransposons are no longer active.

For more information on retrotransposons please visit the Overview page.
